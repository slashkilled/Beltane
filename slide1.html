History of Beltane

Beltane is an ancient Celtic festival that marks the beginning of summer. It was traditionally celebrated on the night of April 30 and the day of May 1.

The festival comes from Ireland and Scotland and was one of the most important events of the Celtic year. People believed it was a time when the natural world became more active and full of life.

Fire was the main symbol of Beltane. Large bonfires were lit to protect people, animals, and crops from harm.
